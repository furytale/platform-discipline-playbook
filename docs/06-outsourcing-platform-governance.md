# Outsourcing Platform Governance

## Core question

What changes when platform reuse lives inside client-owned or outsourcing delivery?

## Why platform code is not enough

A reusable asset asks for adoption, education, compatibility, support, migration, and evolution. In client-owned delivery, that work often becomes hidden because the visible delivery lane funds product output.

## Client delivery vs platform stewardship

Product delivery optimizes for immediate product flow. Platform stewardship keeps reusable capability coherent. Both can be legitimate; the mistake is assigning both silently to the same capacity.

## Funding as governance

If adoption, education, compatibility, support, and evolution are not funded, reduce platform ambition, keep the asset local, treat it as an internal accelerator, or create a funded platform/enabling lane.

## Team topology as architecture

Platform owner, enabling role, stream-aligned product team, and shared stewardship are different responsibilities. The architect can initiate the platform. The platform must eventually be owned by a topology that can operate it.

## Import mechanics as ownership mechanics

A versioned dependency, vendored copy, squashed import, fork, generator output, or manual adaptation each creates a different ownership model.

## When to reduce ambition

Reduce ambition when no one owns support, no one funds compatibility, adoption needs enablement that does not exist, or product assumptions are still too specific.

## What this must not imply

- This is not a universal methodology.
- This is not proof that one platform shape always wins.
- This does not replace local architecture judgment.
- This should be adapted, not adopted blindly.
